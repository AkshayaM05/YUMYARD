Chicken Biryani

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Basmati rice
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 4 green chilies, slit
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Salt, to taste
  • Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 green cardamoms, 4 cloves, 2 cinnamon sticks, 1 star anise

Instructions

  1. Wash the Basmati rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot. Add the whole spices and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Remove half of the onions and set aside for garnishing.
  4. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and sauté for a minute.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and oil begins to separate.
  6. Add the chicken pieces, biryani masala, and salt. Cook on medium heat until the chicken is done.
  7. Add the yogurt, coriander leaves, and mint leaves. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes.
  8. In a separate large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained rice. Cook until the rice is 70% cooked. Drain the water.
  9. In the pot with the chicken, layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. Sprinkle the reserved fried onions on top.
  10. Drizzle ghee over the rice. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal with aluminum foil to trap the steam.
  11. Cook on low heat (dum) for about 20-25 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and flavors are well blended.
  12. Fluff the biryani gently with a fork. Serve hot with raita and enjoy!
